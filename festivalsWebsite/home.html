India celebrates a wide variety of festivals throughout the year, with some of the most prominent being Diwali, Holi, Navratri, Durga Puja, and Ganesh Chaturthi. These festivals are celebrated for diverse reasons, including religious significance, harvest, and cultural events. Many are celebrated nationally, while others are regional. Diwali: Also known as the "Festival of Lights," it symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. Holi: The "Festival of Colors," it marks the arrival of spring, the victory of good over evil, and the love between Radha and Krishna. Navratri: A nine-night festival that celebrates the divine feminine power and victory of goddess Durga. Durga Puja: A major festival in West Bengal and other parts of India that commemorates the victory of goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura. Ganesh Chaturthi: Celebrates the birth of Lord Ganesha and is particularly prominent in Maharashtra. Onam: A harvest festival celebrated in Kerala.
